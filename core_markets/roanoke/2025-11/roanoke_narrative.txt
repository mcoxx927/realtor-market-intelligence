The Roanoke real estate market closed November 2025 with a Market Health Score of 56 out of 100, positioning itself as slightly bullish and tilting toward seller advantage despite some underlying volatility. The market processed 219 transactions against an active inventory of 632 properties, with homes moving at a brisk 24 days on market on average. The average median price settled at $297,101, though this figure masks significant price compression from earlier in the year and notable divergence across submarkets. Among the highest-volume cities, Salem stands out with an impressive 80 health score and just 17 days on market despite a median price of $265,000, while Cave Spring commands $365,000 at 27 days on market with a 60 health score and positive 5.8% year-over-year growth.

The six-month trend reveals a market in transition that warrants careful interpretation. Median prices peaked in June at $366,651 before declining to $307,428 in November, representing a 16% contraction that suggests either a shift in product mix or genuine pricing pressure. Days on market improved from October's concerning 37-day mark back down to 25 days in November, indicating renewed buyer urgency or more realistic pricing. Inventory peaked at 716 units in October before pulling back to 667 in November, a 7% reduction that typically signals tightening conditions heading into the slower winter months. The presence of multiple hot markets alongside isolated buyer markets and several areas requiring attention points to a fragmented landscape where ZIP code selection will be critical over the next quarter.

Sellers should recognize that while the overall market tilts slightly in their favor, pricing discipline is essential. Properties priced competitively are clearing in under four weeks, but overpricing will result in extended market time as evidenced by October's 37-day average. Sellers in Roanoke proper, where the health score sits at 60 and year-over-year prices are up 7.0%, have more pricing flexibility than those in submarkets experiencing double-digit declines. Buyers have gained negotiating leverage compared to six months ago, particularly in neighborhoods showing price deterioration, though they should act decisively on well-priced inventory given the 24-day average absorption. Investors should adopt a market-specific approach: markets exhibiting health scores of 60 or above with stable or positive year-over-year trends favor quick-flip strategies, while areas with depressed pricing but fundamentally sound locations may present hold opportunities for those with longer time horizons and adequate reserves.

Actionable insights: First, concentrate acquisition and listing activity in Salem, which combines an 80 health score with 17-day absorption despite year-over-year price softness, suggesting strong underlying demand. Third, monitor the inventory-to-sales ratio closely through Q1 2026, as November's inventory contraction could accelerate pricing recovery if sustained through spring.
