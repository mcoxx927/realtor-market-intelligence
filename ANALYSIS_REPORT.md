## Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis examines real estate market trends for two major metropolitan areas: **Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia, NC-SC** and **Roanoke, VA**. The data spans over 9 years and reveals significant market shifts, particularly in recent months showing a transition toward buyer-favorable conditions.

### Key Findings:

1. **Both markets experiencing significant inventory buildup** - Active listings up 35-39% YoY
2. **Days on market increasing substantially** - Properties taking 20-29% longer to sell YoY
3. **Price reduction activity surging** - Nearly half of all active listings have reduced prices
4. **Buyer demand moderating** - Pending ratios declining across both markets
5. **Market shift underway** - Transitioning from seller's to more balanced/buyer's market conditions

## Key Insights & Patterns

### 1. Market Transition Underway
Both markets are experiencing a fundamental shift from the seller-favorable conditions of recent years toward more balanced or buyer-favorable dynamics. This is evidenced by:
- Surging inventory levels
- Increasing days on market
- Rising price reduction activity
- Declining pending ratios

### 2. Inventory Normalization
After years of historically low inventory (particularly during 2020-2022 pandemic period), both markets are seeing substantial supply increases:
- **Charlotte:** From pandemic lows around 2,000 active listings to current 10,131
- **Roanoke:** From lows around 250-300 to current 947
- This represents a return toward more historically normal inventory levels

### 3. Pricing Pressure Building
Nearly half of all active listings have undergone price reductions:
- **Charlotte:** 4,912 reduced listings (48.48% of inventory)
- **Roanoke:** 506 reduced listings (53.43% of inventory)
- YoY increases in reductions: +38.21% (Charlotte), +27.14% (Roanoke)

### 4. Absorption Rate Slowdown
Properties are taking significantly longer to sell:
- **Charlotte:** +20.21% YoY increase in days on market
- **Roanoke:** +28.89% YoY increase in days on market
- Both markets now at 58-day median (vs 30-40 days in hot market periods)

### 5. Demand Moderation
Buyer activity shows clear signs of cooling:
- Pending ratios declining double-digits YoY in both markets
- Month-over-month pending listing counts decreasing
- Reduced urgency reflected in longer negotiation periods

### 6. Price Trajectory Divergence
- **Charlotte** maintaining modest price appreciation (+1.98% YoY) despite headwinds
- **Roanoke** experiencing price depreciation (-2.74% YoY)
- Smaller markets like Roanoke appear more vulnerable to demand shifts

### 7. Long-term Price Appreciation Intact
Despite recent cooling:
- **Charlotte:** +39.21% total appreciation since July 2016
- **Roanoke:** +65.45% total appreciation since July 2016
- Recent moderation following years of rapid appreciation

## Conclusion

The real estate markets in Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia, NC-SC and Roanoke, VA are experiencing a significant transition from the seller-dominated conditions of 2020-2022 toward more balanced, buyer-favorable dynamics. This shift is characterized by:

- **Substantial inventory normalization** following pandemic-era lows
- **Moderating demand** as affordability constraints and economic uncertainty weigh on buyers
- **Weakening seller pricing power** evidenced by widespread price reductions
- **Slower transaction velocity** with properties taking 20-30% longer to sell

While both markets face similar headwinds, the **smaller Roanoke market is experiencing more pronounced price pressure**, with year-over-year declines emerging, while **Charlotte maintains modest appreciation** despite challenging conditions.

The **long-term price appreciation trends remain intact** (+39% in Charlotte, +65% in Roanoke since 2016), suggesting the current softening represents a healthy correction and normalization rather than a fundamental market collapse.

**Market participants should adjust expectations** for a continued buyer-favorable environment through at least mid-2026, with the potential for stabilization emerging once inventory and demand dynamics find a new equilibrium.
