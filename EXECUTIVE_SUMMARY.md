## The Four Major Trends

### 1. INVENTORY SURGE (+36-39% YoY)
- Charlotte: From pandemic lows of ~2,000 to current 10,131 active listings
- Roanoke: From lows of ~250 to current 947 active listings
- Supply substantially outpacing demand absorption
- **Impact:** Shift to buyer leverage in negotiations

### 2. VELOCITY SLOWDOWN (+20-29% YoY DOM)
- Properties taking 20-30% longer to sell than prior year
- Both markets converging at 58-day median (vs 30-40 day hot market norm)
- Buyer urgency evaporated
- **Impact:** Sellers must be patient or adjust pricing

### 3. PRICING PRESSURE (48-53% Reduction Rate)
- Nearly half of all active listings have reduced prices
- Charlotte: 4,912 reduced listings (+38.21% YoY)
- Roanoke: 506 reduced listings (+27.14% YoY)
- **Impact:** Sellers lack pricing power, must meet market

### 4. DEMAND MODERATION (Pending Ratios Down 11-13% YoY)
- Buyer activity measurably weakening
- Charlotte pending ratio: 0.491 (below balanced 0.50 threshold)
- Roanoke pending ratio: 0.579 (healthier but declining)
- **Impact:** Reduced competition benefits buyers

## Conclusion

The Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia and Roanoke real estate markets are undergoing a significant **transition from seller-dominated to buyer-favorable conditions**. After years of historically low inventory and rapid price appreciation (particularly during 2020-2022), both markets are experiencing a normalization characterized by:

- **Abundant inventory** (up 36-39% YoY)
- **Weakened seller pricing power** (48-53% reduction rate)
- **Slower transaction velocity** (58 days on market, up 20-29% YoY)
- **Moderating buyer demand** (pending ratios declining double-digits)

While **long-term appreciation trends remain intact** (+39% Charlotte, +65% Roanoke since 2016), the **near-term outlook favors buyers** as inventory normalization continues and demand constraints persist.

**Smaller markets like Roanoke** are experiencing more pronounced price pressure (-2.74% YoY) compared to **larger, more stable markets like Charlotte** (+1.98% YoY), though both face similar macro headwinds.

Market participants should **adjust expectations for a continued buyer-favorable environment through at least mid-2026**, with potential stabilization emerging once inventory and demand dynamics achieve a new equilibrium.
